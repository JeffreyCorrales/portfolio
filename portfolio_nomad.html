Overview


Rail Nomads Camp was one of our largest levels. It was an impoverished outdoor train depot controlled by a tribe of one-armed individuals called the Topekans. This tribe was in a fued with another tribe called the Atchinsons. This fued was a key part of the questline and required a lot of scripting due to the many possible outcomes. One funny outcome resulted in the leader of the Atchisons chopping off his own arm in solidarity with the one-armed Topekans.

Typically dialog heavy RPGs use a dedicated conversation editor, but that was not the case in Wasteland 2. The dialog system was a unique Keyword based approach which was implemented directly in code alongside special markup that could be tokenized by the UI system.

Nomads was home to some of my favorite scripted moments. One was a scenario in which you could knock down a totem pole and use it to rescue a drowning kid, or send it crashing right on top of him. Another was a scenario where you come across a turtle flipped on it's back. You could help it right itself and then watch as it slowly crawled away leading players who were patient enough to follow to a special buried cache.

Nomads was also the location of the game's most popupular NPC companion, a drunk hobo named Scotchmo. I helped implement a running gag throughout the game in which Scotchmo would always be present at any bar the player stumped across.
