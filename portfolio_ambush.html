Overview


Ambush site was the most important level I was entrusted to own, as it was the very first level in the game. Everyone knows that if you can't get the player to buy in during the first 30 minutes of gameplay then they are most likely going to quit.

My work primarily consisted of coming up with a layout that allowed for proper scaffolding of gameplay mechanics along with scripting encounters that introduced the pillars of Wasteland: multiple solutions to problems, choice and consequence, and moral ambiguity.

Straight out of the openeing cinematic the player finds themselves on a narrow peninsula of ice surrounded by enemies and forced to fight for their lives. The player follows the frozen pathways to a hostage situation, where their choices could result in an additional party recruit. One of the fan favorite moments of the entire game occurs just as the player is getting ready to leave Ambush site. The player can rescue a trapped cat, Major Tomcat, and gain an animal companion.

Throughout all the various encounters I implemented tutorials and metric events to teach the player the basics and analyze where they might be struggling.

Given the level's complex mix of terrain and jagged ice sheets I created a navmesh editor tool that let us paint and erase voxels that conformed to the terrain's heights. The voxels blocked nav mesh generation and thus become a mold for clean navmesh generation within the desired walkable space. The interface let us save and load paint jobs so that they could be easily shared among designers.
