Overview


Highpool was a level which showcased world reactivity. Shortly after completing the tutorial level the player recieved a distress call in which they're forced to make a choice between responding to Highpool or Ag. Center. Whichever they choose to ignore ended up getting destroyed.

A lot of the reactivity was achieved via our radio broadcast system. I implmeneted numerous radio broadcasts that were cries for help from desperate civilians, status checks from Ranger HQ, and quest reminders from key NPCs. These broadcats made the world feel alive and allowed the player to feel the wieght of their actions.

Wasteland 2 had very little safety rails for the player. They were allowed to kill any NPC and so design had to aware of handling every possible outcome. I tracked the player's hostile actions towards NPCs via our faction rep system and this altered whether the player was liked or hated, which in turn affected conversation choices and quest outcomes.

Part of the Highpool questline involved repairing damage to the dam's water pumps. I scriped a light in-world puzzle which required the player to split out their party up into two groups. One group manned circuit breakers which opened doors in the underground lab, while the other group ventured into the lab and fought off enemies to repair broken valves.
